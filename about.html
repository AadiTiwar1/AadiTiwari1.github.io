The following is my CommonApp essay. I wrote it in 2024 in the midst of high school college applications so it may seem cheesy as it was trying to appeal to admissions officers, but feel that to this day it continues to do a pretty good job at describing my lifelong interests in Quantum Physics & Computing and why I'm pursuing what I am.

Some students have a background, identity, interest, or talent that is so meaningful they believe their application would be incomplete without it. If this sounds like you, then please share your story.

While many people treasure important moments or days in their lives, I treasure my most creative thought, one I had at 7 years old: how cool would it be to live in a world with teleporters? I started daydreaming about how much better the world would be with teleporters: no more late slips at school, no more pollution, meeting friends whenever I wanted. Wondering why one didn’t exist in real life, I was led down a rabbit hole of Wikipedia pages with words I hardly understood until I found the page for Quantum Physics.

I remember reading the Quantum Physics page and immediately being captured - things like quantum entanglement and tunneling seemed straight out of a fantasy movie, even though I couldn't understand any of it. Weeks of reading later, I put the article down hoping to eventually understand this magical field I’d stumbled upon in greater depth.

A few years later in 9th grade, the computing aspect to my Quantum Physics obsession began. Growing up in the competitive Silicon Valley computer science environment, I was always told that becoming a software engineer would solve any problem I’d ever have in life. Admittedly, the problem-solving aspect of computer science was unique and it had real world applications. It struck a chord with me during a time when I was forced to take mandatory classes that didn’t feel useful. Yet, computer science didn’t feel like something I could uniquely call my own. It made me feel like another cog in the gears of silicon valley, a clone of everyone else. I became disinterested, desperate for something that was unique and enjoyable.

A few months later, I came across a news article: “Quantum Computing: The Technology of the Future.” It described taking the wondrous properties of quantum physics I once wanted to master as a child and fusing it with the problem-solving and applicability of computer science to create something that I could call mine, something that’d allow me to pursue my childhood dreams. I realized that Quantum Computing was my calling. Lit with the fire of a newfound passion, I was set on immersing myself as deeply into the world of quantum computing as a high schooler possibly could.

Immediately, no other topic taught at school fascinated me nearly as much as this one did. It felt like every new fact about superposition or matrix representations of a quantum logic gate got me one step closer to harnessing a superpower; an unparalleled feeling. During a time where I was struggling with school and needed motivation to go on, some proof that I could succeed at anything, I had Quantum Computing. It proved to me that anything I truly set my mind to, I can learn.

I’d start sleeping later and later learning more and more as I tried to learn to cast the spells of this magic called quantum physics. And eventually, I was a quantum wizard. The magical properties which I was so spellbound by as a child made sense to me, and it now seemed like I could do anything. I started to get compliments from PhDs in the field, asked to preview a book about Quantum Machine Learning, and finally felt like I’d started making it when a UCLA professor saw enough in me to take me on as a first-author for an official research paper last summer.

I continue to pursue Quantum Physics & Computing as it's not just a 7 year old's daydreams anymore - it's the dreams of me yesterday, today, and tomorrow. There is so much room for innovation and creativity in this nascent field, and I strive to one day be remembered as an innovator in the field.
